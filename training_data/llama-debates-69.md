"Responsibility?" Mia raised an eyebrow. "You call doing math problems until midnight responsible? That's just not healthy. And it's not like our teachers even care if we get it done or not – they just want to see the grade on the paper."

"That's not true," Alex protested. "Our teachers are just trying to help us learn and succeed. Without homework, we wouldn't be challenged to think critically and apply what we've learned in new ways."

Mia sighed. "I don't know, Alex. I just feel like there are better ways to spend our free time than on boring math problems or reading a 200-page history textbook. We could be learning new things, exploring interests... who knows, maybe even starting our own businesses!"

Alex chuckled. "You're always thinking outside the box, Mia. But this isn't about creativity – it's about basic literacy and numeracy skills."

Mia leaned in, her voice rising. "Those can be taught in class, just like every other subject! Why do we need to take that stuff home with us? It's not like we're going to use most of what we learn in real life anyway... unless we become accountants or something."

Alex smiled wryly. "Hey, someone's got to keep the economy running, right?"

The two friends continued to argue back and forth, neither convincing the other, but both enjoying the lively debate.

Mia laughed and clapped him on the back. "That's what makes life interesting, right?"

Their friends at nearby tables began to stir, curious about the commotion. One of them called out, "Hey, guys! Can you make it a friendly debate? Loser buys coffee!"

Alex and Mia exchanged a mischievous glance before Alex stood up, his arms spread wide in challenge.

"Game on," he said with a grin.
